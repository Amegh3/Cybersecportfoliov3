Cyber Hygiene Checklist

Best Practices for Cyber Hygiene

Follow these practices to significantly enhance your cybersecurity posture:

  • Use Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA): Add an extra layer of security by requiring additional verification methods beyond just a password.
  • Regularly Update Software: Keep all your software, including operating systems and applications, up-to-date to protect against known vulnerabilities.
  • Backup Data: Regularly back up important data to ensure you can recover it in case of a ransomware attack or data loss.
  • Secure Your Network: Use firewalls and encrypt your Wi-Fi network to prevent unauthorized access.
  • Educate Yourself and Others: Stay informed about the latest cybersecurity threats and educate your family or colleagues to recognize and respond to potential threats.

Online Banking Fraud

How Scammers Exploit:

Scammers often use phishing emails, fake websites, or malware to gain unauthorized access to individuals' online banking accounts.

Example of a Scam:

An attacker sends an email that appears to be from a bank, asking the recipient to verify their account information via a provided link. The link directs to a fake banking website where login credentials are captured by the scammers.

Example Hacker Message:


Message: "URGENT: Your account has been compromised. Please click the link below to secure your account immediately. Failure to do so will result in account suspension."
Details: This message often includes a link to a fraudulent site that attempts to capture your login credentials.

Prevention Tips:

  • Verify Emails: Always check the sender's email address and avoid clicking on links in unsolicited messages.
  • Use Secure Websites: Ensure the URL begins with "https://" and verify the site's authenticity before entering any personal information.
  • Enable Two-Factor Authentication: Add an extra layer of security to your banking accounts.

Recovery Method:

If you fall victim to online banking fraud, immediately contact your bank to report the incident. Change your account passwords, monitor your account for unauthorized transactions, and consider running a security scan on your devices.

Details: This message is typically accompanied by instructions on how to pay the ransom and may include a countdown timer.

Cryptocurrency Scams

How Scammers Exploit:

Scammers exploit the popularity of cryptocurrency by creating fake investment opportunities or fraudulent exchanges to steal money from investors.

Example of a Scam:

A fake cryptocurrency investment platform promises high returns and lures investors to transfer funds to a wallet address controlled by the scammers. Once the funds are transferred, the scammers disappear.

Example Hacker Message:


Message: "Congratulations! You've won a cryptocurrency prize. To claim your winnings, send a small fee to the provided wallet address. Hurry, the offer is limited!"
Details: Such messages often promise large rewards in exchange for a small upfront payment, which is a common tactic used in scams.

Prevention Tips:

  • Verify Platforms: Research and verify the legitimacy of cryptocurrency platforms before investing.
  • Be Cautious: Avoid investment opportunities that promise guaranteed high returns with little risk.
  • Secure Transactions: Use secure and reputable wallets and exchanges for cryptocurrency transactions.

Recovery Method:

If you fall victim to a cryptocurrency scam, report the incident to the relevant authorities and the platform. If possible, alert your bank or cryptocurrency exchange. Be cautious with future investments and verify the credibility of opportunities before proceeding.

Job Offer Scams

How Scammers Exploit:

Scammers create fake job offers to trick individuals into providing personal information or paying fees for job applications or training.

Example of a Scam:

A fake company offers a high-paying job with minimal qualifications and asks the candidate to pay for background checks or training materials before starting the job.

Example Hacker Message:


Message: "Congratulations! You've been selected for a high-paying job position. To secure your spot, please send a processing fee of $200."
Details: This message often promises an attractive job opportunity but requires payment or personal information upfront.

Prevention Tips:

  • Verify Job Offers: Research the company and validate job offers before providing any personal information or making payments.
  • Avoid Paying Fees: Reputable employers do not ask for payment for job applications or training.
  • Contact Employers Directly: Reach out to the company through official channels to confirm the legitimacy of job offers.

Recovery Method:

If you encounter a job offer scam, report it to the relevant job boards and authorities. Monitor your financial accounts for any unusual activity and take steps to protect your personal information.

Social Media Scams

How Scammers Exploit:

Scammers use social media platforms to spread fraudulent schemes, phishing links, or fake giveaways to steal personal information or money.

Example of a Scam:

A scammer creates a fake social media profile and posts a link to a supposed contest or giveaway, asking users to enter their personal details to claim the prize.

Example Hacker Message:


Message: "Congratulations! You've won a free gift card. Click the link below to claim your prize and provide your details to receive it."
Details: This message often leads to a fake site designed to capture personal information or financial details.

Prevention Tips:

  • Be Skeptical: Avoid clicking on suspicious links or sharing personal information through social media.
  • Use Privacy Settings: Adjust your social media privacy settings to limit access to your information.
  • Report Suspicious Activity: Report any scams or suspicious messages to the social media platform.

Recovery Method:

If you fall victim to a social media scam, immediately change your passwords, report the scam to the platform, and monitor your accounts for any unusual activity. Consider contacting relevant authorities for further assistance.

Email Phishing Attacks

How Scammers Exploit:

Scammers use phishing emails to trick individuals into providing personal information or credentials by pretending to be a trustworthy entity.

Example of a Scam:

A phishing email appears to come from a trusted organization, asking the recipient to click a link and log in to verify their account details.

Example Hacker Message:


Message: "Alert: We noticed unusual activity on your account. Click the link below to secure your account and verify your identity."
Details: The link leads to a fake login page that captures user credentials.

Prevention Tips:

  • Check Email Addresses: Verify the sender's email address before clicking on links or providing information.
  • Use Security Software: Utilize anti-phishing tools and software to detect and block phishing attempts.
  • Educate Yourself: Be aware of common phishing tactics and how to identify suspicious emails.

Recovery Method:

If you fall victim to a phishing attack, change your passwords immediately and notify your email provider. Monitor your accounts for any unauthorized activity and consider running a security scan on your devices.

Fake Tech Support Calls

How Scammers Exploit:

Scammers impersonate tech support representatives and trick individuals into providing remote access to their devices or paying for unnecessary services.

Example of a Scam:

A scammer calls claiming to be from a well-known tech company, stating there is an urgent issue with the recipient's computer that requires immediate attention. They request remote access or payment for services.

Example Hacker Message:


Message: "Your computer has a critical security issue. Call us immediately to fix it. Our technicians are available 24/7 to assist you."
Details: This message aims to create a sense of urgency and convince the victim to grant remote access or make payments.

Prevention Tips:

  • Verify Calls: Contact the company directly using official contact information if you receive unexpected support calls.
  • Avoid Remote Access: Do not grant remote access to anyone unless you initiated the contact and verified their legitimacy.
  • Report Scams: Report any suspicious tech support calls to relevant authorities.

Recovery Method:

If you have given remote access or made a payment to a scammer, immediately run a security scan on your device, change your passwords, and report the incident to relevant authorities.
